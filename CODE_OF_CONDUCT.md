# Code of Conduct

**Hearth** (working codename) is an open community. We want participating in it — filing an issue, opening a
pull request, joining a discussion, writing a module or theme — to be a respectful, harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This document is referenced by [GOVERNANCE.md §6](GOVERNANCE.md), which defines who enforces it.

## 1. Our standards

**Behavior that builds the community:**

- Showing empathy, patience, and kindness toward others.
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences, and assuming good faith.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback; focusing on what is best for the project and the
  wider community.
- Taking responsibility for our mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and learning from them.

**Behavior that is unacceptable:**

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without their explicit
  permission ("doxxing").
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## 2. Scope

This Code applies in all Hearth project spaces — the code repositories, issue trackers, pull requests,
discussion forums, chat, and any official event or channel — and also when an individual is officially
representing the project in a public space (for example, using an official account or acting as an appointed
representative).

## 3. Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the **Conduct team** at
**`conduct@<your-project-domain>`** *(placeholder — set this address during project setup; until it exists,
contact the Lead Maintainer privately, per [GOVERNANCE.md §6](GOVERNANCE.md))*.

Reports are handled **confidentially**. Please include, if you can: what happened, where and when, who was
involved, any context or records (links, screenshots), and whether the situation is ongoing. You do not need
to have a perfect account to report — tell us what you can. A member of the Conduct team who is involved in
or has a conflict of interest in a report will recuse themselves from handling it.

## 4. Enforcement

The **Conduct team** (per [GOVERNANCE.md §6](GOVERNANCE.md) — initially the Lead Maintainer plus one other
Maintainer) reviews every report, acknowledges receipt as promptly as is practical, investigates fairly, and
decides on a response. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code in good faith may face temporary or
permanent repercussions as determined by the rest of the Maintainer group.

Responses follow this **enforcement ladder**; the team selects the step that fits the severity and pattern of
the behavior, and may begin at any step:

1. **Correction.** A private, written notice explaining the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate.
   A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning.** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. The individual must avoid
   interaction with those involved — including unsolicited contact and project spaces — for a specified
   period. Further violations may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.
3. **Temporary ban.** A time-limited ban from any interaction or public communication within the project.
   No public or private contact with those involved is permitted during this period. Violating these terms
   may lead to a permanent ban.
4. **Permanent ban.** A permanent ban from any kind of public interaction within the project community.

Records of reports and actions are kept confidentially to inform future decisions and to recognize patterns
of behavior.

## 5. Appeals

Anyone subject to an enforcement action may appeal it. Appeals are decided by the **full Maintainer group**
(excluding any Maintainer with a conflict of interest), whose decision is final. To appeal, contact the
Maintainers through the reporting address above, stating the action and why you believe it should be
reconsidered.
